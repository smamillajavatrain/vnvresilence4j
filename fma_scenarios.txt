DR & HA MAPPING TEMPLATE (Disaster Recovery and High Availability)
RTO Recovery Time Objective
RPO Recovery Point Objective

| Abbreviation | Full Form                    | Meaning                                                                                                                                                 |
| ------------ | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **RTO**      | **Recovery Time Objective**  | The maximum acceptable time to restore a system/service after a failure or disaster. Example: If RTO = 2 hours, system must be restored within 2 hours. |
| **RPO**      | **Recovery Point Objective** | The maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time. Example: If RPO = 15 minutes, you can afford to lose only last 15 minutes of data.         |
| **DR**       | **Disaster Recovery**        | The strategy and processes to recover IT systems, data, and infrastructure after disasters like hardware failure, cyber attack, or natural disasters.   |
| **HA**       | **High Availability**        | System design approach that ensures services remain continuously operational with minimal downtime using redundancy, failover, clustering, etc.         |



DR Mapping Table
| Component    | HA Strategy          | DR Strategy          | RTO    | RPO         |
| ------------ | -------------------- | -------------------- | ------ | ----------- |
| REST Service | Multi pod deployment | Redeploy from image  | 15 min | 0 data loss |
| Database     | Aurora cluster       | Cross region replica | 30 min | 5 min       |
| OCP Cluster  | Multi node           | Secondary cluster    | 1 hour | Minimal     |
| A3P          | Redundant endpoints  | Failover routing     | 30 min | Minimal     |

RTO = 1 hour → Application must be restored within 1 hour after outage

RPO = 5 minutes → Maximum 5 minutes of transaction data loss allowed

DR → Backup data center or cloud recovery setup

HA → Load balancers + multiple servers running simultaneously to avoid downtime


Understand the Purpose of FMA
FMA identifies:

	Possible failure scenarios

	How failures are detected

	Impact on application/business

	Recovery or mitigation strategy
	
Objective:

	Improve resiliency

	Reduce downtime

	Define monitoring + alert strategy

	Define auto-recovery and manual recovery actions
